STYLISTIC GUIDELINES FOR OUR PROJECTS

"Type" is only hyphenated if it's followed by a noun. So, "Pikachu is an Electric type. His Lightning Speed is a Normal-type move. This move is not very effective against Rock-type Pokémon."
The word "region" is lower-case when used on its own, upper-case when following the region name (example: Alola Region).
Use commas when writing out numbers with four or more digits (example: 100,000 Volts).
Words like "sir" and "ma'am" should be lower-case unless they're part of a title or part of the address at the start of a letter.
Family names should be upper-case when addressing a specific person ("Hi, Gramps!").
"Mythical Pokémon" is used as a translation of maboroshi no pokemon for generation 4 content onwards, save for Mewtwo Strikes Back Evolution. For generations 1 and 2, "Phantom Pokémon" is used. Either is usable for generation 3.
Moves and ability names are upper-case, but the words "move" and "ability" should be lower-case.
Stats and natures should be lower-case.
"Goodbye," not "Good bye."
"All right," not "Alright."
Exclamation and question marks only go inside quotation marks when the quotated word or phrase is an exclamation or a question, respectively.
Periods and commas go inside quotation marks unless there's an exclamation or question mark already, in which case they go outside if they can't be omitted.
All punctuation directly following or preceding italicized words is to be italicized.
Outside of edge cases, italics are only used for emphasis, to help convey inflection, and/or to denote when a line is being delivered by three or more characters.
Always use spoken name order (example: Okido Yukinari, Nariya Okido).
